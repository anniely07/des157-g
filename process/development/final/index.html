Rejecting Our Cultures

Growing up, many Asian American children reject their culture in attempts to fit in or avoid bullying. Ethnic foods are seen as gross and/or smelly while names are butchered and made fun of to the point a child adopts an english name.

"I'm sorry for giving you a weird middle name"

Job Opportunities

Many Asian Americans have a harder time getting better job positions because of the stereotype that Asians (especially East Asians) are more subordinate than dominant.

Experiences of Racism

“Yellow fever” or the asian fetish. The western perception has often been the Asian women are subservient, obedient, and family oriented -- making them the perfect wife. This can damage their self worth and make them unsure if someone is really interested in them or their race. It can also endanger women because they are seen as something like an object that the white man can chase after. Not really a person, but just an asian.

Asian Americans are also known as a model minority -- successful law abiding citizens that don’t rely on a welfare check, but on their own hard work. This idea of asians as a model minority was thanks to white media. This was just an excuse to indirectly scold and justify their racism against black people. If Asian people are successful people without the help of the government, then why can’t black people do it too? It wasn’t as if every Asian person became successful this way. Furthermore a century before, Asians were labeled as inferior, but now they are at “equal” standing when it was to white people’s better interest (stopping black people from “taking” so much government resources). This model minority myth also harms Asian Americans and causes divide against other minorities. Racial justice groups are more often likely to ignore Asian plight because of this reinforced image of Asians being successful.
